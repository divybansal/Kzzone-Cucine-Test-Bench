Prefect Finishes

LAMINATE MATT FINISH

A laminate with a duller satin or frosty finish cuts down on glare and the reflectivity that could disrupt a scanner. Generally a semi-gloss or matte finish is more subtle, looks sharper and makes colors appear deeper and brighter.

LAMINATE HI-GLOSS

The glossy, reflective finish grabs attention and implies a richer product. Hi Gloss laminate is durable in that it repels dust, dirt and fingerprints. Even when it does come into contact with surface dirt, it is easily wiped clean. Gloss is the standard for trade paperbacks.

ACRYLICS HI-GLOSS

Acrylic wood named in English is Hi Gloss Acrylic, and in Vietnam, commonly known as gloss wood, or Gloss Acrylic Wood, is a very popular product for modern luxury furniture. Product Application: It is a high glossy acrylic sheet which is perfectly ideal for kitchen shutters, bathroom cabinets, wardrobe & other interior applications.

SATIN MATT FINISH

Satin. This silky, smooth paint finish actually retains its pearl-like sheen when dry. It is also a good indicator of stain resistance and how washable a coat of paint will be satin finish is much more forgiving of holes and patches.

PU HI-GLOSS

PU HI Gloss is weather resistant and quick drying. It gives excellent result when applied on interior and exterior of natural wood & wood veneer. Hi Gloss has non-yellowing property also. It should be stored at ambient conditions in well ventilated location and away from source of ignitions and fire.

PU MATT FINISH

PU Matt polish is medium range polish available in variety of sheen. It can be applied on various wood like metallic surfaces and non-metallic surfaces too. On wood the PU Matt polish that is applied is clear polish that brings shine to natural wood

LACQUERED GLASS

Lacquered glass, colored and opaque in appearance, is produced by depositing and then baking a coating of lacquer to one side of clear glass. Lacquer is a solution of film-forming materials, natural or synthetic, usually applied as an ornamental or protective coating.

DESIGNER/TINTED GLASS

Tinted glass can be used to form a curtain wall, and is most often used in commercial applications, offices, and other large facilities. Tinted glass helps to regulate a building's temperature as it is designed to absorb energy from the visible light spectrum.
